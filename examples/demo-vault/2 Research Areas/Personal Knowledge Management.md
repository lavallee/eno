# Personal Knowledge Management

Personal knowledge management (PKM) is the practice of capturing, organising, and
retrieving what you learn so that past effort compounds instead of evaporating.
A good PKM system is less an archive than a thinking partner.

## The capture problem

Most notes die at capture. We highlight, clip, and screenshot, then never return.
The fix is to make capture cheap but review deliberate — a lightweight inbox that
is emptied on a schedule rather than a graveyard that grows forever. Some call the
end state a [[Second Brain]].

## Linking over filing

Rigid folder hierarchies force a note into exactly one place. Associative linking,
as practised in [[Zettelkasten]] and other note systems, lets a note live in many
contexts at once. The value of a note grows with the number of good connections
into it, not the tidiness of its folder.

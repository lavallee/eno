# Synthesis - Spaced Repetition and Active Recall

A short synthesis note connecting two techniques that are often confused.

## The distinction

[[Active Recall]] is the *act* of retrieving information from memory without looking.
[[Spaced Repetition]] is the *schedule* that decides when those retrievals happen.
One is the exercise; the other is the training plan. Neither works nearly as well
alone.

## Why the pairing compounds

A retrieval that lands right at the edge of forgetting is both harder and more
valuable than an easy one. Spacing manufactures that difficulty on purpose, and
active recall converts it into durable memory. Framed as a loop, this is just
[[Deliberate Practice]] applied to remembering: targeted effort, immediate feedback,
expanding challenge.

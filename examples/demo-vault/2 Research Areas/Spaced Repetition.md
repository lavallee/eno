# Spaced Repetition

Spaced repetition schedules reviews at expanding intervals, timing each review to
land just before you would have forgotten. It is the practical engineering answer
to the way human memory decays.

## Why it works

Each successful recall at the edge of forgetting strengthens the memory trace and
lengthens the next safe interval. The technique pairs naturally with
[[Active Recall]]: the spacing decides *when* to test, active recall decides *how*.

## Diminishing effort, growing retention

Because intervals expand, a mature deck costs only a few minutes a day while
holding thousands of facts near-permanently. The discipline it rewards is the same
one behind [[Deliberate Practice]] — small, targeted, repeated effort beats
occasional cramming.

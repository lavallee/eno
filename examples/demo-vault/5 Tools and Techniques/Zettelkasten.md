# Zettelkasten

A Zettelkasten ("slip-box") is a note system built from small, atomic notes that
each hold a single idea and link explicitly to related ideas. It grew out of
analogue index-card practice and adapts cleanly to digital tools.

## Atomic notes

Each note states one idea in your own words and stands on its own. Atomicity is what
makes linking meaningful: a note about one thing can be referenced precisely, rather
than buried inside a sprawling document. This is the backbone of most modern
[[Personal Knowledge Management]] systems.

## Links as thought

The value of the box is in its connections. Writing a note forces you to ask what it
connects to, and that question — not the note itself — is where thinking happens. Over
time the web of links becomes a map of what you actually understand.

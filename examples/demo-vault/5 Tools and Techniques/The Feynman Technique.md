# The Feynman Technique

The Feynman Technique is a method for finding the holes in your own understanding:
explain the idea in plain language as if teaching a beginner, and watch for the
places where you reach for jargon or hand-wave. Those places are the gaps.

## The loop

Pick a concept, explain it simply, notice where the explanation breaks, then go back
to the source and repair the gap. Repeat until the explanation is clean. It is
[[Deliberate Practice]] for comprehension — each pass targets a specific weakness
rather than vaguely "reviewing".

## Why plain language works

Jargon lets you move symbols around without understanding them. Plain language does
not, so it exposes borrowed knowledge. Writing the explanation into a
[[Zettelkasten]] note makes the repair permanent.

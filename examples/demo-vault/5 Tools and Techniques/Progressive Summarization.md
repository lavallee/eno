# Progressive Summarization

Progressive summarization is a layered method for distilling notes so their most
valuable parts surface over time. On first read you save a source; on later passes
you bold the best passages, then highlight the best of the bold, compressing the note
toward its essence with each visit.

The point is to defer the work of distillation until you actually need the note,
rather than over-processing everything up front. Done well it feeds a
[[Second Brain]] with material that is already skimmable. A [[Commonplace Book]] is
the analogue ancestor of the same idea. Some practitioners fold the
[[Feynman Technique]] in at the final layer, rewriting the distilled note as a plain
explanation.
